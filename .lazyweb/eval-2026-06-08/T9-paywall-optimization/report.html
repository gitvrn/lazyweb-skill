Current friction (the conversion thesis). On a cold first session a niche fasting app has three live frictions: (1) trust — "is this app legit and will it work for me?"; (2) trial anxiety — "will I get charged today / surprise-billed?"; (3) price legibility — the annual plan is defaulted but its value vs monthly and its per-day cost are invisible, so "annual" reads as "expensive" rather than "best deal." The hypotheses below each attack one named friction with a distinct mechanism.

4 Redesign hypotheses

Four falsifiable hypotheses, each attacking a different named friction with a distinct mechanism. Form: making [change] should [outcome] because [mechanism]. Metrics name a primary lever and a guardrail, mirroring the A/B Agent's target_metric / guardrail_metric structure.

H1

Add a "no charge today" trial-mechanics timeline

Replacing the bare "7-day free trial then $X/yr" line with an explicit today → day-5 reminder → day-7 charge timeline plus a "no payment due now" badge should raise trial-start rate (and net paid conversion) because cold first-session users hesitate at a hard paywall mainly out of fear of a surprise charge — making the no-cost window and the reminder concrete removes that specific anxiety.

Concrete change
Below the CTA, add a 3-step vertical timeline: Today — full access, $0 charged · Day 5 — reminder notification before billing · Day 7 — trial ends, annual plan begins, cancel anytime. Add a small "No payment due now" pill on/near the CTA. Optionally pre-schedule the day-5 reminder and confirm it inline (BoldVoice pattern).
Friction addressed
Trial anxiety / surprise-charge fear on a cold session.
Expected metric
Trial-start rate ↑  guardrail: trial→paid conversion & refund/support volume must not fall
Supporting evidence
  • mimo [Lazyweb · PRO A/B] — extended trial + explicit annual pricing on the CTA surface; learning: a clearer trial + payment expectation "can reduce commitment anxiety." exp 4b363766 · conf high
  • BetterMe, Rays, Lose It, Quizlet, mimo, Facetune [Lazyweb] — the today/day-5/day-7 timeline + "no payment due now" is a widely-shipped convention, including in Health & Fitness.
Risk: a billing timeline makes the charge more salient and can suppress trial starts among the most price-sensitive. Treat learning as directional; this is the lowest-novelty bet, so run it as a clean A/B before layering other changes.
H2

Surface an annual-vs-monthly comparison with a per-day reframe

Replacing the single defaulted annual plan (monthly hidden behind "more options") with two visible plan rows — annual pre-selected and badged "Best value / Most popular," reframed as "~$X/mo, billed yearly" next to a deliberately less attractive monthly row — should lift annual-plan share (without cutting overall conversion) because users only perceive the annual discount when they can see the monthly price it beats; an invisible comparison reads as "expensive," a visible one reads as "obviously the better deal."

Concrete change
Show both plans on the paywall (no extra tap). Annual row pre-selected, with a "Best value" badge, a "Save NN%" chip, and a per-month/per-day breakdown ("$6.99/mo, billed annually"). Keep monthly visible but plain. Name the default in the CTA: "Start free trial → Annual". Hold the annual price steady while keeping monthly full-price so annual looks relatively better.
Friction addressed
Price legibility — the annual value vs monthly is currently invisible.
Expected metric
Annual-plan share ↑  guardrail: overall paid-conversion rate must hold
Supporting evidence
  • dawn [Lazyweb · PRO A/B] — held annual steady while raising the weekly price; learning: this "makes the annual plan look relatively more attractive." exp 79bf0ed7 · conf high
  • lockwidget [Lazyweb · PRO A/B] — swapping weekly for a lower-commitment monthly option "anchors users toward a higher annual price." exp 1e83e388 · conf high
  • Zero [Lazyweb] — its own "more billing options" sheet badges annual $69.99 "most popular" with a per-month savings line — the exact reframe, in a fasting app.
  • Grammarly, Washington Post, Speak, MyFitnessPal [Lazyweb] — visible annual/monthly with "best value" badge and an explicitly annual CTA is the dominant convention.
Risk: exposing monthly can let undecided users pick the cheaper monthly entry point, denting annual share if the annual framing isn't strong enough. Mitigate by keeping annual the visual default and the per-day delta large.
H3

Productize the benefits into fasting-specific outcomes

Replacing the generic benefits list with a short, productized list of fasting-specific outcomes ("Personalized fasting windows," "Fasting-streak tracking," "Glucose & weight trends," "Coach-built protocols") should raise paid conversion because a cold user on a niche app converts on a concrete picture of what the subscription does for them, not on broad "Premium / VIP" language — outcome-named features make the value legible before the price.

Concrete change
Cut generic lines; keep 4–5 outcome-named rows, each with an icon and a one-line payoff tied to fasting (windows, streaks, glucose/weight trends, protocol library, Apple Health sync). Optionally end with an "…and more" catchall to imply breadth without a wall of text (Carrot pattern). Lead the headline with the outcome, not the offer.
Friction addressed
Trust / value clarity — "what exactly do I get, and is it for my fasting goal?"
Expected metric
Paid conversion ↑  guardrail: scroll-depth / CTA-tap rate (don't bury the CTA)
Supporting evidence
  • reddit [Lazyweb · PRO A/B] — moved from broad VIP language to a productized perk list; learning: "a productized feature list can clarify Premium value better than broad VIP language." exp 825f3dd8 · conf high
  • apple-fitness [Lazyweb · PRO A/B] — benefit-led activation copy "sells the workout outcome before the subscription terms." exp 47238428 · conf high
  • gentler [Lazyweb · PRO A/B] — broader, expanded benefit story "can support higher pricing." exp 40106370 · conf high
  • Rootd, Zero, MyFitnessPal [Lazyweb] — checkmarked, outcome-named benefit lists are the fitness-paywall norm (Zero: "personalized stats … custom plans").
Risk: over-specific claims a cold user can't yet verify can read as hype and erode trust. Keep claims concrete and demonstrable; pairs well with H4's third-party proof.
H4

Add a social-proof & credibility band

Adding a compact credibility band — a star rating, a fasted-with-us user count, and one short outcome testimonial — directly above the CTA should raise paid conversion because a niche app carries almost no brand trust on a cold first session, and third-party proof substitutes for the trust the brand can't supply, lowering the "is this legit / will it work for me" barrier at the decision point.

Concrete change
Insert a single-row proof band: ★ rating + count ("4.8 · 12k ratings"), a "Trusted by N fasters" stat, and one rotating 1-line testimonial naming a concrete result. Optional press/award badge if real. Keep it to one screen-row so it doesn't push the CTA below the fold.
Friction addressed
Trust deficit specific to a niche / low-brand app on first session.
Expected metric
Paid conversion ↑  guardrail: time-to-CTA / bounce — proof must not crowd out the offer
Supporting evidence
  • I Am Sober [Lazyweb] — post-onboarding habit-app paywall leads with community count + 4.9 rating + testimonial: the exact pattern for a niche behavior-change app.
  • Breathwrk, MindLlama, Genie, Zero [Lazyweb] — "users who started premium" counts, award/star badges, 5-star testimonial carousels, and social-proof logos recur across Health & Fitness paywalls.
  • Honesty note: no A/B experiment in the returned set isolates a social-proof band as its variable, so H4 is visual/convention-grounded, not experiment-backed. Strong prior, weaker evidence than H1–H3.
Risk: fabricated or thin proof backfires and can trip App Store review — use only real numbers. Adding urgency instead of proof (countdown/last-chance) is a separate, more aggressive lever; see Divergent options, and guard refund/chargeback rates if tested.

6 Divergent option (bolder, transfers from adjacent categories)

D1

Offer a "skip the trial, start annual now" express path

For high-intent users, add a secondary, clearly-labelled "Skip free trial — start annual today" option (a small link/row, not a competing button) that converts straight to the paid annual plan, optionally sweetened with a small first-purchase discount. Mechanism transferred from fitness-challenge and language apps: a visible trial-bypass removes a redundant step for users already sold, and an explicit annual discount nudges them past it — without removing the trial path for everyone else.

  • justfit [Lazyweb · PRO A/B, Health & Fitness] — countdown plan selector with explicit skip-free-trial copy; learning: "urgency, plan comparison, and clear trial bypass can reduce purchase friction when users are deciding whether to subscribe now." exp 258ef49b · conf high
  • drops [Lazyweb · PRO A/B] — leading with a lifetime anchor while pre-selecting a discounted yearly trial "can make the annual trial feel cheaper and easier to start." exp fae32674 · conf high
  • apple-music [Lazyweb · PRO A/B] — concrete value + acceptance-language CTA makes plan selection "feel like claiming value before comparing prices." exp bfd1aa04 · conf high
Risk / why it's divergent, not default: a trial-bypass can cannibalize trial starts (and the trial's downstream retention benefit) if it's too prominent, and added urgency raises refund/chargeback exposure. Ship it small and guard trial-start volume + refund rate. This is a deliberately bolder bet than H1–H4.
