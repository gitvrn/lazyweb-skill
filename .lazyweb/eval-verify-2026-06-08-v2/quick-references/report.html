FOR THE CODING AGENT

Build the streak-freeze / "save" buffer first — it is the best-evidenced pattern here and it converts the dreaded loss screen into a recoverable, non-punitive moment.

LAZYWEB REPORT — AGENT HANDOFF
Use this report as a starting point for building the streak-loss + streak-recovery screens (loss screen, freeze/repair offer, milestone celebration) using these grouped real-app references as a visual baseline.

TOP RECOMMENDATIONS (do first):
1. Ship a streak-freeze / "save" buffer the user holds or earns BEFORE they can lose a streak (Mimo "Streak Repair/Freeze" store, Drops "rescues", Alma "streak saves", Duolingo "earn streak freezes"). This is what makes the loss screen humane.
2. Make the loss screen recovery-first, not shame-first: state the break plainly, then offer ONE primary "get back on track" action (Gentler "lost your streak → do a quick workout"; Quizlet "study to restart the streak").
3. Reward re-engagement with a milestone celebration — confetti + a single share/continue CTA at meaningful thresholds (Flo 100 days, Me+ 28 days, Finch/Falou/Couple-Joy day-1).

INDEX ON: streak-freeze/repair/save economy as the dominant recovery mechanism (5 of 11 streak screens reviewed); milestone celebration with one CTA (5 of 11); single-primary-action recovery copy.
DO NOT OVER-INDEX ON: the explicit "you lost your streak" full-screen as a standalone pattern — only 2 of 11 streak references showed one, so treat its exact layout as directional, not settled. Ignore the generic % / countdown discount paywalls that surfaced on "save/offer" queries; those are monetization screens, not streak recovery.

Patterns

1 · Give the streak a freeze / "save" buffer so loss is recoverable, not terminal

Highest leverage and best-evidenced moment in this corpus: the strongest apps don't just announce the loss — they pre-arm the user with a consumable freeze/repair/save so a missed day costs a token, not the whole streak. Proven across 5 of 11 streak references (Mimo, Drops ×2, Alma, Duolingo). Build this before the loss screen — it is what lets the loss screen be kind.

Pattern B — Streak freeze / repair / save economy

Strong (for this set) seen in 5 of 11 streak references Directional

The recovery mechanism the best apps lead with: a consumable the user earns, buys, or is gifted that absorbs a missed day. It appears as a store item ("Streak Freeze / Streak Repair"), an inventory of "rescues," or a "streak saves" counter with a "how it works" link. Pre-arming the user is what turns a broken streak into a token spend instead of a reset to zero.

Pattern C — Streak-milestone celebration

Directional seen in 5 of 11 streak references Directional

A full-bleed celebratory overlay at a threshold (day 1, then larger milestones) — big number/flame or confetti, a weekly tracker, motivational copy, and one primary CTA (Continue / Share / reflect). The most consistent visual language in the corpus; reuse it as the reward when a recovered user re-engages.

Pattern A — Recovery-first "you lost your streak" screen

Directional · thin seen in 2 of 11 streak references Directional

When a dedicated loss state does appear, the strong version is recovery-first, not shame-first: state the break plainly, keep the tone gentle, and lead with ONE "get back on track" action rather than dwelling on the failure. Note the thin evidence (2 of 11) — many apps lean on the freeze buffer (Pattern B) and never show a standalone loss screen.
